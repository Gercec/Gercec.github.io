John stood on the second floor of Vitebsky railway station in St. Petersburg leaning against the wrought iron railings. Cold twilight light fell through huge stained glass windows painting the empty hall in blue tones. John was just waiting for his train scrolling through his phone. Suddenly from the old baggage department a man in early 20th century railway uniform emerged. He carried a heavy leather suitcase and strangest of all his steps on the marble floor made no sound.
John followed him onto the open platform under a giant metal canopy. The man sat on a bench right under the station clock. John noticed something impossible: the hands above the stranger's head began spinning wildly backwards! Gathering courage John approached. "Excuse me" he said "something is wrong with the clock." The man raised his head and John saw his eyes glowing with soft amber light. "Time here is just a decoration John" he rasped.
The stranger took an old brass key from his pocket and placed it on the edge of the bench. "My time has come your turn" he whispered and simply dissolved in a cloud of steam from a passing locomotive. John blinked and the platform was empty the clock showing the correct time again. The station attendant asked John why he was standing alone in a closed sector. John looked at his palm: there lay a heavy worn key.
